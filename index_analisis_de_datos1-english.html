About the project

Welcome to the Cyclistic bike-share case study! In this case study, I will be working as a data analyst for the company Cyclistic. I have been provided with a massive dataset containing over 6 million records. To effectively analyze it, I will be using tools such as SQL, R y Tableau. Let's get started!

About Cyclistic

Cyclistic is a ficticious bike-sharing company launched in Chicago in 2016. Its marketing strategy is based on flexible pricing that includes single-ride passes, day passes, and annual memberships. Financial analysts have concluded that the future growth of the company will depend on maximizing the number of annual members.

The marketing team's goal is to convert casual riders into annual members. To achieve this, they need to study the differences between the two groups and how digital media can affect their strategy. An analyst is assigned to the team to examine historical bike trip data and help create an effective marketing strategy.

Actions taken

  1. Define the business task: The first step was to examine the input from stakeholders to determine what the business task was.
  2. Data study:The next action was to study the properties of the data (type and characteristics) and its context (metadata and origin). This way it was determined that the data was of good quality and did not contain bias.
  3. SMART Questions: The SMART questions that were expected to be answered through this data analysis project were defined next. These questions provided a guide of what needed to be analyzed.
  4. Scope Of Work: A SOW was created to define the purpose of this data analysis project, the activities to be carried out, the project timeline with time estimates, the deliverables, and the project scope.
  5. Data preparation: The data was then prepared, ensuring that it was in the appropriate format for later work. Additionally, the data was filtered and classified for better cleaning in the next phase.
  6. Data cleaning and processing: The next activity was data cleaning where duplicates, null data, and errors were searched for. Then, data was separated and merged where considered appropriate.
  7. Record of data treatment: A document was also created that recorded all relevant transformations that were made to the data before starting the analysis.
  8. Data analysis: With the data ready to work with, various data analysis, statistical, and data visualization tools were used. Some of these tools included R, Tableau, and SQL. Observations of the findings were made to subsequently include them in the project report.
  9. Report generation and results presentation: Finally, a report was created that was presented to stakeholders with the project findings. Additionally, relevant recommendations were made based on what was found during the analysis.

Business task

The purpose of this project is to study the historical bike trip data of Cyclistic's different customer types (casual riders and members), particularly over the last 12 months, to understand how they use Cyclistic bikes differently.

The results obtained in this project will be key for the marketing team to determine the most effective digital marketing strategies. These strategies will allow them to connect with Cyclistic's audience and influence casual riders to opt for an annual membership.

About the data

Data context

  • Who: The person or organization that created, collected, and funded data collection.
    -Lyft Bikes and Scooters, LLC
  • What: That in the world in which the data can have an impact.
    -The data can help us analyze bike rides and allow us to make better decisions to improve the quality of life of our users.
  • Where: The origin of the data.
    -The data was obtained from the Cyclistic website.
  • When: The moment when the data was created or collected.
    -The data is created periodically every month.
  • Why: The motivation behind the creation or collection.
    -To better understand Cyclistic users.
  • How: The method used to collect the data.
    -Cyclistic users require using an app to rent bicycles, from which travel data is collected.

Data properties

Each trip is anonymous and includes:
  • Trip ID
  • Vehicle type (classic bike, electric bike, docked bike)
  • Start date and time
  • End date and time
  • Start station ID
  • Start station name
  • End station ID
  • End station name
  • Start station latitude
  • Start station longitude
  • End station latitude
  • End station longitude

Discoveries

What was the percentage distribution of bike preferences?



Observations
  • Member preferences equal: Member travelers do not have a significant preference for a type of bike since their distribution was almost 50%/50%.
  • Preference for electric bikes: Casual travelers have a higher preference for electric bikes than for classic bikes.
  • Some casual travelers use docked bikes: Although none of the member travelers used docked bikes, some casual travelers did use them.

How is the service usage distributed over the week?



Observations
  • Casual travelers travel more on weekends: It is observed that the volume of casual trips decreases on weekdays and increases on weekends.
  • Member travelers travel more on weekdays: The opposite pattern is observed with member travelers who use the service more frequently during the week.

How is the service usage distributed over a day?



Observations
  • Same peak hour: The peak hour for the start of casual and member user trips is around 5:10 PM.
  • Variable behavior patterns during the day: Member travelers have an initial peak around 8:30 AM, then a decrease until 4:00 PM when it rises again. In contrast, casual travelers have a continuous increase in departures from the morning until the peak hour.

What is the average trip duration?



Observations
  • Longer duration for casual trips: The average duration of casual trips (22:52 mins) is longer than that of member trips (12:27 mins).
  • Distinct average duration peak: It is observed that the highest volume of casual trips occurs at 7 minutes, while for member trips, the highest volume occurs at 4 minutes.
  • High dispersion: A higher dispersion is visualized in the histogram of casual trips, which confirms why the average is so high.

What is the preferred station for starting trips?



Observations
  • Significant preference in casual travelers: A significant preference is perceived in casual travelers, almost 25% of trips started at the "Streeter Dr & Grand Ave" station.
  • No significant preference found in member travelers: It is observed that in the pie chart for members, trips are distributed almost equitably for all stations.
  • Similar pattern in trip ending: When graphing for trip ending, the same pattern was found for both types of travelers.

How are the trip start points distributed geographically?



Observations
  • Similarity in geographic behavior: A significant difference in the behavior of both types of travelers is not perceived.
  • High usage of services in a specific radius: It can be perceived that there is a radius in which a lot more trips are initiated than in other geographic locations.
  • Similar pattern in trip completion: When graphing for trip completion, the same pattern was found for both types of travelers.
