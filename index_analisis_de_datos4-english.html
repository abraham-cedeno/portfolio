About the project

Welcome to the data analysis project for IFood! In this project, we will analyze data from a food delivery company in Brazil. The reason for the project is the need for the marketing department to spend their annual budget more intelligently. The CMO perceives the importance of taking a more quantitative approach when making decisions.

A small team of data scientists was hired with a clear goal: to build a predictive model that would support direct marketing initiatives. Ideally, the success of these activities will demonstrate the value of the approach and convince the skeptics within the company. We will mainly use the R tool to build the model.

About Ifood

IFood is a food delivery companythrough an app that operates in the food retail sector. It has several hundred thousand registered customers and serves almost one million consumers per year. They sell products from 5 main categories: wines, rare meat products, exotic fruits, specially prepared fish, and sweet products. These can also be divided into "gold" and regular products.

Globally, the company had solid revenues in the last 3 years, but las perspectivas de crecimiento no son prometedoras... For this reason, several strategic initiatives are being considered, one of which is to improve the performance of marketing activities, with a special focus on marketing campaigns.

Actions taken

  1. Definition of the business task: The first thing we did was to examine the input of the stakeholders to determine what the business task was.
  2. Data study: The next action was to study the properties of the data (type and characteristics) and its context (metadata and origin). This way, it was determined that the data was of quality and did not have bias.
  3. SMART Questions: Then, we defined the SMART questions that we expected to answer through this data analysis project. These questions provided a guide to what we wanted to analyze.
  4. Data preparation: We then proceeded to prepare the data, ensuring that the data was in the appropriate format to work with later. In addition, the data was filtered and classified for better cleaning in the next phase.
  5. Data cleaning and processing: The next activity was data cleaning, where duplicates, null data, and errors were sought. Then, data was separated and merged where appropriate.
  6. Data analysis: With the data ready to work, different data analysis, statistical, and data visualization tools were used. Among some, we can mention: R, tableau, and SQL. Then observations were made of the findings to subsequently append them to the project report.

Business task

The business task is to "build a predictive model that produces the highest gain for the next marketing campaign, scheduled for next month." The new campaign, the sixth, aims to sell a new device to the customer database.

About the data

To build the model, a pilot campaign involving 2,240 customers was conducted. The customers were randomly selected and contacted by phone for the acquisition of the device. The total cost of the sample campaign was 6,720 MBR, and the revenue generated by customers who accepted the offer was 3.674 MBR.

Globally, the campaign had a benefit of -3.046MBR. The success rate of the campaign was 15%. Hopefully, the model will allow the company to choose customers who are more likely to buy the offer and leave out those who did not respond, making the next campaign highly profitable.

Statistical Analysis

With 28 data points from each client in this section, we will show some of the discoveries obtained from statistical data analysis. If you want to know more details about the characteristics that do not appear in this section, visit the link at the beginning of this page ("R project URL").

Is the difference in conversion rates for customers with teenagers vs customers without teenagers statistically significant?



Statistical Test Conclusions
  • Chi-square test result: The p-value is 0.0004998, indicating strong evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore, we conclude that the result is statistically significant.
  • Potential Predictor: The results suggest that whether customers have teenagers at home or not could be a potential predictor for the predictive model we will build.

Is the difference in income medians statistically significant?




Statistical Test Conclusions
  • Permutation test result: The p-value is 0, indicating strong evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore, we conclude that the result is statistically significant.
  • Potential Predictor: The results suggest that income could be a potential predictor for the predictive model we will build.

Is the difference in conversion rates for users with complaints vs users without complaints statistically significant?



Statistical Test Conclusions:
  • Permutation test result: The p-value is 31.6%, indicating weak evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore, we conclude that the result is not statistically significant.
  • Not a useful predictor: The results suggest that complaints do not function as a potential predictor for the predictive model we will build.

Is the difference in conversion rates for customers with more web visits per month vs customers with fewer web visits per month statistically significant?



Statistical Test Conclusions:
  • Chi-square test result: The p-value is 0.0004998, indicating strong evidence against the null hypothesis. Therefore, we conclude that the result is statistically significant.
  • Potential Predictor: The results suggest that whether customers visit the website more or less in a month could be a potential predictor for the predictive model we will build.
